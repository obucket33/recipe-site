A Slice Of History

History of New York style Pizza The New York style slice grew out of Neapolitan style pizza when Italian immigrants brought pizza to NYC—and America—in the early 1900s. New York style pizza has slices that are large and wide with a thin crust that is foldable yet crispy. ... Pizza without additional toppings is called “plain,” “regular,” or “cheese.”

Originally, this style of pizza was baked in wood-fired ovens. However, when gas-fired ovens became a more afforable option, more chefs made their pizza this way and continue to. By using a gas-fired oven, a unique flavor was included in the pizza. In the beginning of New York Pizza, people garnished their pizza with cheese, oil, anchovies, and garlic.
